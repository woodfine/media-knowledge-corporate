Investment covers the structural mechanics of direct-hold limited partnership investment.
Articles in this category describe how the LP legal form implements the direct-hold
architecture, the securities law eligibility framework governing who may subscribe, how
each asset vehicle is legally isolated from others in the programme, the mechanics of
the ICR-gated distribution declaration process, and how commercial mortgage financing is
sized and structured at the asset level.

Content is structural and procedural — how the direct-hold LP investment mechanism works
in practice, not the terms of any specific offering or the projected outcomes of any
particular asset. All articles are written for an institutional finance reader with
familiarity with Canadian securities law and commercial real estate investment structures.
