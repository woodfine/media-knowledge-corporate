Woodfine Capital Projects Inc. maintains ongoing disclosure obligations under Canadian securities
law as a reporting issuer. This section organises the company's public filings, governance
documents, corporate communications, and investor engagement materials into a structured
reference that allows investors, legal counsel, and other stakeholders to locate and download
current materials.

The disclosures in this section are governed primarily by National Instrument 51-102
*Continuous Disclosure Obligations*, which prescribes the filing deadlines, document formats,
and content requirements applicable to reporting issuers in Canada. All filings are made
electronically through SEDAR+ (the System for Electronic Document Analysis and Retrieval Plus)
and are accessible to the public at sedarplus.ca.

## Coverage

WCP operates as the parent company and is the reporting issuer. Each of the four direct-hold
solutions operates under WCP's consolidated reporting umbrella. Where material, separate
disclosure is provided for individual solutions at the level required by applicable regulations.

## Regulatory framework

Canadian continuous disclosure obligations are established by the Canadian Securities
Administrators under the umbrella of National Instrument 51-102. The instrument requires
reporting issuers to file annual and interim financial statements, an annual information form,
and management's discussion and analysis on a schedule tied to their fiscal year end. Material
changes must be disclosed promptly by press release, followed by a formal report on Form
51-102F3. Business acquisition reports are required for significant acquisitions meeting the
prescribed significance tests.

OSC Staff Notice 51-721 addresses the electronic filing of continuous disclosure documents
and the technical requirements for SEDAR+ submissions. All WCP filings follow the requirements
of that notice and the associated SEDAR+ system specifications.

## Fiscal year

WCP's fiscal year ends December 31. Annual disclosure documents are due within 90 days of
fiscal year end (March 31). Interim disclosure documents for each of the first three quarters
are due within 45 days of the applicable quarter end.
